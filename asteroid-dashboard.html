Torino Scale (0–10): An integer scale for communicating impact hazard to the public. 0 = zero or negligible chance of collision. 10 = certain global catastrophe. The vast majority of monitored objects score 0.

Palermo Technical Impact Hazard Scale (logarithmic): Combines probability and energy of a potential impact relative to the background rate of similar events. Values below −2 = well below background risk. Values between −2 and 0 = merit attention. Values above 0 = events posing greater-than-average risk — rare and closely watched.
